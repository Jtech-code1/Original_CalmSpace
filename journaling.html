How to Start Journaling

1. Find a quiet space where you won’t be disturbed.

2. Set a timer for 5–10 minutes to keep your practice simple.

3. Write freely without worrying about grammar or structure.

4. Reflect on your emotions, thoughts, or events of the day.

5. End with a positive note — gratitude, an affirmation, or a hopeful thought.

Benefits: Journaling improves self-awareness, reduces stress, helps process emotions, and strengthens clarity in decision-making.

Journaling Prompts & Techniques

📝 Gratitude Journal

Write down 3 things you’re grateful for each day.

Benefit: Builds positivity and rewires your brain to focus on joy.

🌅 Morning Pages

Write 1–2 pages of unfiltered thoughts first thing in the morning.

Benefit: Clears mental clutter and sparks creativity.

🌙 Evening Reflection

Record your highlights, lessons, and challenges of the day.

Benefit: Encourages mindfulness and better sleep.

💡 Prompt Journaling

Examples: “What made me smile today?” or “What do I need to let go of?”

Benefit: Unlocks deeper insights and emotional release.
